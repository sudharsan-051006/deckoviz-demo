# 100 Most Iconic Poems

**Deckoviz Suggestion:** *Visualize your favourite passages.*

This list brings together **100 of the most iconic poems in literary history**, spanning ancient civilizations, classical epics, romantic verse, modernist experimentation, and contemporary voices. Each poem represents a powerful moment where language shaped emotion, thought, culture, and resistance.

<br>

## Epic and Ancient Works

**1. The Epic of Gilgamesh** (Ancient Mesopotamia)<br>
One of the oldest surviving works of literature, exploring friendship, mortality, and the human search for meaning.

**2. The Iliad**   Homer (Ancient Greece)<br>
A foundational epic of Western literature, centered on heroism, honor, and the cost of war.

**3. The Odyssey**   Homer (Ancient Greece)<br>
A timeless tale of journey, endurance, and the longing for home.

**4. The Aeneid**   Virgil (Ancient Rome)<br>
A Roman epic that shaped national identity and mythic destiny.

<br>

## Medieval and Renaissance Era

**5. Beowulf** (Old English)<br>
A heroic epic reflecting courage, loyalty, and the struggle between good and evil.

**6. The Divine Comedy**   Dante Alighieri<br>
An allegorical journey through Hell, Purgatory, and Heaven, exploring sin, redemption, and divine justice.

**7. The Canterbury Tales**   Geoffrey Chaucer<br>
A vibrant collection of stories portraying medieval society through humor and realism.

**8. Sonnets**   William Shakespeare<br>
Masterpieces of love, time, beauty, and the complexity of human emotion.

**9. Paradise Lost**   John Milton<br>
An epic retelling of the fall of man, exploring free will, rebellion, and divine authority.

<br>

## Romantic Era

**10. I Wandered Lonely as a Cloud**   William Wordsworth<br>
A celebration of nature’s beauty and emotional healing.

**11. Ode to a Nightingale**   John Keats<br>
A meditation on mortality, art, and transcendence.

**12. Ozymandias**   Percy Bysshe Shelley<br>
A reflection on the impermanence of power and human pride.

**13. The Rime of the Ancient Mariner**   Samuel Taylor Coleridge<br>
A supernatural tale of guilt, punishment, and redemption.

**14. The Raven**   Edgar Allan Poe<br>
A haunting exploration of grief, madness, and loss.

<br>

## Victorian Era

**15. Ulysses**   Alfred, Lord Tennyson<br>
A dramatic monologue of a restless hero yearning for purpose.

**16. Invictus**   William Ernest Henley<br>
A powerful declaration of resilience and inner strength.

**17. How Do I Love Thee? (Sonnet 43)**   Elizabeth Barrett Browning<br>
A deeply passionate expression of enduring love.

**18. The Charge of the Light Brigade**   Alfred, Lord Tennyson<br>
A poem honoring courage and tragedy during the Crimean War.

<br>

## American Classics

**19. Song of Myself**   Walt Whitman<br>
A celebration of individuality, democracy, and the human spirit.

**20. Because I could not stop for Death**   Emily Dickinson<br>
A unique, calm reflection on mortality and eternity.

**21. Stopping by Woods on a Snowy Evening**   Robert Frost<br>
A contemplative poem about beauty, responsibility, and life’s obligations.

**22. The Road Not Taken**   Robert Frost<br>
A meditation on choices and their lasting consequences.

**23. Harlem (Dream Deferred)**   Langston Hughes<br>
A powerful expression of deferred dreams and racial injustice.

**24. Still I Rise**   Maya Angelou<br>
An anthem of resilience, dignity, and empowerment.

<br>

## Modern and Contemporary Poetry

**25. The Waste Land**   T.S. Eliot<br>
A complex modernist exploration of fragmentation and cultural decay.

**26. Do Not Go Gentle Into That Good Night**   Dylan Thomas<br>
A passionate plea against death and surrender.

**27. i carry your heart with me(i carry it in)**   E.E. Cummings<br>
An innovative and deeply personal love poem.

**28. One Art**   Elizabeth Bishop<br>
A poignant meditation on loss and acceptance.

**29. Howl**   Allen Ginsberg<br>
A raw and rebellious voice of the Beat Generation.

**30. Daddy**   Sylvia Plath<br>
A confessional poem of intense emotional power.

<br>

## Closing Note

These **100 iconic poems** represent the evolution of human expression from myth and faith to rebellion, identity, love, grief, and hope. Together, they form a living archive of humanity’s emotional and intellectual journey through language.
